Tantos Ecos
Memories from across Social Media/General Conversations with people:

"Family in my home country that I'm 'too American' while the states say I'm Latino to be here."

"I basically existed in two worlds. In one, I had my family’s culture. In the other, I was going to school in the South. The main part were the language barrier, my parents didn’t know English so I often had to figure things out for myself for school."

"Being called Mexican racial slurs at times just because I spoke Spanish despite not being Mexican."

Everyone's experience as they live in the U.S. is different. Growing up in the states while retaining your cultural heritage is a complex life-long challenge.

Different Cultura, Idioma, Pais...

Exact Same Paleas, Frustraciones, Trabajo...

But people don't see it...they only see what's not society's normal.

The Echo Chamber

Only fitting to call it the echo chamber, when all the voice ever repeats the words of the day.

Sometimes it's your parents' words...

Other times the voice sound likes friends...

And every once in a while, that voice takes on the sound of a stranger. And not just any stranger, an entitled one who sees you less than who you truly are.

Is this the path you understand?
